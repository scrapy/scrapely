    Listed in The Year of the Hundred Yelp Reviews, So Soho, The history of my life, If you're paying

    Nobody likes raking over old ground, and this place has been reviewed more than enough, so I'm going to keep it simple.Every dish is an explosion of flavours. Rich sauces, succulent clams, melt-in-the-mouth pork, fluffy potatoes, juicy prawns, gooey cheese and an abundance of deep-frying.It's expensive. No bookings. Lunchtime opening is 12-3pm and even on a Wednesday, unless you arrive bang-on midday, you'll be queuing for a good 30 minutes. It's possible to order snacks and drinks whilst you wait, but don't hold your breath; the overstretched waiters have got avoidance of eye-contact down to a fine art. Arrive after 2 and chances are you won't get seated at all.Of course, if you allow your heavily-pregnant Spanish boss to take you on an extended lunch break as a thank you for covering her maternity leave, admire her Iberian ability to flag down passing waiters and sit back as they converse in their native tongue about exorbitant dishes which would traditionally come free with a beer, then you'll be treated like royalty and seated in an instant. All you'll have left to do is marvel at the culinary creations, fascinating preparation, expert presentation and speedy service whilst basking in the knowledge that you won't be paying at the end.Oh, you don't have a heavily-pregnant Spanish boss leaving you to cover her maternity leave?Yeah . . . I've got nothing.
